🎓 Beginner Progression Plan
11 steps to sharpen your ear

Stage 1 — Get Your Bearings

1
Start with C Major, Octave 4. Press ▶ Play Scale and listen to the full scale several times until it feels familiar. Your ear needs to know the terrain before it can navigate it.
2
Turn on "Play reference first". This plays the root note before each mystery note, giving your ear a tonal anchor. Leave it on until you feel confident without it.
3
Run your first Note Names quiz. Click ▶ Start Quiz with Note Names mode selected. Don't worry about your score — you're training your ears, not testing them. Yet.

Stage 2 — Build Consistency

4
Aim for 20 answers without resetting. Stay with C Major until you can answer consistently. The weighted system keeps surfacing the notes you miss most often.
5
Add G Major. Change Root to G, keep the Major scale. It sounds nearly identical — can your ear spot the difference?
6
Try Natural Minor (Root: A). A minor is the relative of C Major and sounds darker. Compare the two moods back to back.

Stage 3 — Expand Your Palette

7
Try Interval mode. You'll hear two notes and identify the interval between them. Start with Major or Pentatonic Major where intervals are distinct.
8
Enable a timer (5–20s). A little pressure sharpens your instincts. Try to answer before the ring runs out — timeouts are learning moments.
9
Try Pentatonic Minor (Root: A or E). The scale behind most rock and blues. Switch to Guitar or Bass to see the shapes on the fretboard.

Stage 4 — Fluency

10
Explore Chord mode. Identify chord qualities (major, minor, 7th, etc.) built from the current scale. Use Major or Natural Minor for the richest chord variety.
11
Turn off "Play reference first". Now your ear must identify notes and intervals with no anchor. This is where genuine relative pitch lives.
💡 Short daily sessions (10–15 min) beat long occasional ones. Your ear is a muscle — train it a little every day.
📖 How Ear Trainer Works
Features & functionality guide

Configuration

Root Note sets the tonic. Scale / Mode selects from 15 scales. Octave shifts the register — Octave 4 contains Middle C. Range (1–3 Octaves) expands the note pool across additional octaves above the starting octave; the piano visualiser widens to match. With Guitar, Bass, or Ukulele active, a Tuning selector appears with alternative tunings.

Press ▶ Play Scale or ▼ Descending to hear the scale. Clicking either button while the scale is already playing re-triggers it from the start. Use ⚄ Randomise for a surprise combination — tuning is never randomised.

Visualiser

  • 🎹 Piano — Two-octave keyboard. Highlighted keys are in your scale. Click any key to hear it.
  • 🎸 Guitar — 6-string fretboard with 8 tuning options, 13 frets.
  • 🎸 Bass — 4-string fretboard (also 5-string option) with 4 tuning options, 15 frets.
  • 🪗 Ukulele — 4-string fretboard with 5 tuning options, 12 frets.

During a quiz, the visualiser shows ■ green for correct and ■ red for wrong. Clicking the wrong note on the visualiser during an active question counts as a miss.

Quiz Modes

  • Note Names — Hear a single note and identify it from the scale. Toggle "Play reference first" to hear the root before the mystery note.
  • Intervals — Hear two notes and name the interval between them. A reference note (always different from the answer) is played first when the toggle is on. Options only include intervals that exist in your chosen scale.
  • Chords — Hear a chord built diatonically from the scale and identify its quality (major, minor, dominant 7th, etc.). All chord tones are highlighted on the visualiser after you answer.

Sound & Timer

  • Sound — Synth 1 (default: percussive noise burst + sine, bright), Synth 2 (triangle + harmonic, classic), or Synth 3 (warm organ-like sine with sub-octave).
  • Play reference first — Note Names: plays root. Intervals: plays the reference note before the mystery note. Not used in Chord mode.
  • Timer — Off, 5s, 10s, 15s, or 20s. The countdown ring turns red at 3 seconds. Timeouts count against accuracy.

Score & Reset

The score row shows ✓ correct, ✗ wrong (including visualiser misclicks), ⏱ timeouts, and accuracy %. Pressing ✕ Reset shows a summary, saves the session to history, then clears everything for a fresh start. Press 📊 in the toolbar to view your session accuracy chart.

Settings are remembered

All settings — root, scale, octave, tuning, visualiser, quiz mode, theme, toggles — are saved in your browser automatically. No account needed.

🎯 Use the Replay button if you missed the sound. Switch to Piano view while learning to connect sounds with the keyboard layout visually.
