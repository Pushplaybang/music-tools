📖 How Instrument Tuner Works
Features & guide

Pitch History

The scrolling trace shows your pitch deviation over the last ~4 seconds. The centre dashed line is perfect pitch (0 ¢); the faint green band is the ±2 ¢ in-tune zone. The trace is drawn in green when within ±5 ¢ and accent colour when further out. The oldest portion fades to transparent on the left for a clean visual trail. Gaps appear when no pitch is detected.

Needle Meter

The needle shows your current tuning offset in cents (100 cents = 1 semitone). The green zone in the centre means you're within ±2 cents — professional tuning accuracy. The needle is smoothed to reduce jitter; the text readout below uses the raw detected value for precision.

Reference Tone

Press 🔊 Play in the Target box to hear the target note through your speakers. Useful for double-checking pitch by ear without a separate reference instrument. The button glows while the tone plays and is disabled to prevent stacking.

Input Level

The thin bar directly below the pitch trace shows your microphone input volume. Grey = silent, accent colour = good level, red = too loud and may cause inaccurate pitch readings. Aim to keep it in the accent range.

Autodetect Mode

When Autodetect nearest note is on (default), the tuner automatically identifies the closest note to what it hears and measures your offset from that note — no need to select a string. When off, it measures offset from your manually selected target string/note only.

Instrument & Tuning

Select your instrument (Guitar, Bass, Ukulele) and a tuning preset. The string buttons let you lock onto a specific string's target pitch. In Manual mode, use the Root Note and Octave selectors to target any note.

Microphone

Press the microphone button to start listening. Your browser will ask for mic permission. Press again to stop.

Calibration

A4 is set to 440 Hz by default. Adjust with the ± buttons or slider if you need to tune to a different concert pitch (e.g. 432 Hz, 443 Hz for orchestral use).

In-tune LED

The glowing dot (top-left of the display) lights up when you're within ±2 cents of your target note.
