📖 Chord & Scale Reference
How to use this tool

Configuration

Use the Root Note dropdown to select a root note (C through B, including enharmonic names like C#/Db). Choose a chord type or scale from the second dropdown.

Chords View

Select Chords mode to explore 17 chord types including triads (Major, Minor, Dim, Aug, Sus2, Sus4), seventh chords (Maj7, Dom7, Min7, Half-Dim7, Dim7, MinMaj7), and extended chords (Add9, Maj9, Min9, 6, Min6).

The piano keyboard highlights chord tones across 2 octaves with interval labels. The guitar fretboard shows up to 3 common voicings as chord diagrams.

Scales View

Select Scales mode to explore 15 scales across Major Modes, Minor Scales, Pentatonic & Blues, and Symmetric & Other categories. The piano and guitar fretboard highlight all scale degrees.

Diatonic Chords

For Major and Natural Minor scales, the tool displays triads and 7th chords built on each scale degree with Roman numeral analysis (e.g. I, ii, iii, IV, V, vi, vii°). Click any chord pill to jump to the Chords view with that root and type selected.

Guitar Voicings

Guitar chord diagrams are generated algorithmically for standard tuning (EADGBE). The algorithm finds voicings within a 4-fret span and ranks them by: root note in the bass, number of strings used, position on the neck, and open strings. Up to 3 distinct voicings are shown.

Audio Playback

Use ▶ Play Chord to hear all chord tones simultaneously, or ▶ Arpeggiate to hear them one at a time. In Scales mode, play scales ascending or descending. Click individual piano keys or guitar fretboard dots to hear single notes.

💡 All settings including root note, chord/scale type, mode, and theme are saved automatically and restored on your next visit.
