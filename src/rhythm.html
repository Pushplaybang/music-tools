Rhythm — Strumming Patterns

Learn and practice guitar strumming patterns, grooves, and rhythmic feels across multiple genres.

Reading the Grid

  • ▼ — Downstroke (strum towards the floor)
  • ▲ — Upstroke (strum towards the ceiling)
  • × — Mute (damp strings with palm)
  • ◆ — Tap (a lighter percussive hit, used in fingerpicking patterns)
  • · — Rest (silent — skip this beat)
  • Larger / bolder symbols are accented (play louder)
  • Numbers below show beat positions (1, 2, 3, 4…)
  • The + symbol marks the "and" of each beat

Play Along Mode

The pattern loops continuously with a click track. Match your strumming to the highlighted grid position. Start slow, then increase BPM as you get comfortable.

Call & Response Mode

The tool plays the pattern for a set number of bars (the "call"), then goes silent for one bar (the "response") while the grid shows faded positions. Play the pattern from memory during the silent bar. Great for building muscle memory.

Practice Tips

  • Start at a slow tempo (60–80 BPM) and increase gradually
  • Tap your foot on each beat to lock in the pulse
  • Count subdivisions aloud: "1 and 2 and 3 and 4 and"
  • Keep your strumming hand moving in a constant down-up motion, even on rests
  • Gradually reduce the pattern volume to test your internalization
  • Use the genre filter to focus on one style at a time
