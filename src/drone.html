📖 How to Use the Drone
Features & guide

What Is a Drone?

A drone is a sustained reference tone that plays continuously. Musicians use drones to practise intonation, play scales against a tonal centre, match vocal pitch, and hear intervals against a fixed reference.

Use Cases

  • Intonation practice — play your instrument over the drone and train your ear to hear when notes are in tune with the root
  • Scale practice — set the drone to a root note, then play scales over it to internalise the sound of each scale degree
  • Vocal pitch matching — sing along with the drone to develop pitch accuracy
  • Interval practice — use the drone as a fixed reference to practise recognising and singing intervals

Voicings

  • Root — a single sustained note
  • Root + 5th — the root plus a perfect fifth above (7 semitones), creating a stable open harmony
  • Power — root one octave below, the fifth, and the root — a full, powerful sound
  • Octaves — the root plus the root one octave above for a rich, doubled tone

Timbres

  • Warm — sawtooth oscillator through a lowpass filter with a slow LFO modulating the cutoff. Smooth and mellow.
  • Tanpura — detuned sawtooth pairs with a subtle sub-octave sine and slow amplitude tremolo. Shimmering and organic.
  • Organ — sine wave with 2nd and 3rd harmonics. Clean and stable, no modulation.
  • Pad — triangle wave through a lowpass filter with slow LFO. Soft and ambient.

Controls

Select a Root Note and Octave, choose a voicing and timbre, then press the large play button to start the drone. Adjust the volume slider at any time. All parameters can be changed while the drone is playing — crossfades happen automatically with no clicks or pops.
