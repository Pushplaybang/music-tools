🎓 Your Ear Training Journey
A progression plan for beginners
🎵 You don't need to read music or know theory. All you need are your ears and a little patience. Start at Stage 1 and move on only when it feels easy.

Stage 1 — Get Comfortable with Sound

1
Pick the C Major scale (Root: C, Scale: Major, Octave 4)This is the most familiar scale — it matches the white keys on a piano. Press ▶ Play Scale a few times and just listen. Notice how each note feels different.
2
Watch the Piano visualiser while it playsSee which keys light up. Try clicking individual keys to hear them. Repeat until you recognise the "do-re-mi" sound.
3
Start the quiz — no timerClick ▶ Start Quiz with the timer off. Take your time. Enable Play root first so you hear the home note before the question — it gives your ear a reference point.

Stage 2 — Build Your Ear

4
Aim for 80% accuracy on C MajorDo 20–30 questions per session. Wrong answers will appear more often automatically — lean into them. When you hit 80%, you're ready to move on.
5
Add the G Major scaleChange Root to G and keep the Major scale. Notice it sounds almost identical — that's the point. Can your ear spot the difference?
6
Try the Natural Minor scale (Root: A)This is A minor — the relative minor of C Major. It sounds darker and more melancholic. Compare it with C Major and notice how the mood shifts.

Stage 3 — Expand Your Palette

7
Turn on the 10 second timerA little pressure sharpens your instincts. Try to answer before the ring runs out. Don't worry if you miss — timeouts are learning moments.
8
Try Pentatonic Minor (Root: A or E)This is the scale behind most rock and blues guitar. If you play guitar, switch to the Guitar visualiser and you'll instantly recognise the patterns on the fretboard.
9
Use the ⚄ Randomise buttonOnce you feel confident, randomise your scale and root each session. Unknown territory is where real ear training happens.

Stage 4 — Fluency

10
Work through all the modesDorian, Phrygian, Lydian, Mixolydian. Each has a unique emotional character. Look them up, listen to music that uses them, then quiz yourself.
11
Turn off "Play root first"Now your ear has to identify the note with no reference. This is where you build genuine absolute-pitch recognition within a key context.
💡 Short daily sessions (10–15 min) beat long occasional ones. Your ear is a muscle — train it a little every day.
📖 How Ear Trainer Works
Features & functionality guide

Configuration

The top card controls what you're working with. Root Note sets the starting note of the scale (e.g. C, G, F#). Scale / Mode selects from 15 scales including all major modes, pentatonic, blues, whole tone and diminished. Octave shifts the register up or down — Octave 4 includes Middle C. If you're using the Guitar or Ukulele view, a Tuning selector appears with common alternative tunings.

Press ▶ Play Scale to hear the scale ascending, ▼ Descending to hear it in reverse. Use ⚄ Randomise to get a random root and scale for variety — tuning is never randomised.

Visualiser

Four views are available. Switch between them using the tab buttons — the active visualiser always reflects your current scale and quiz state.

  • 🎹 Piano — Two octave keyboard. Highlighted keys are in your chosen scale. Root notes glow brighter. Click any key to hear it.
  • 🎼 Staff — Treble clef notation with correct key signature, ledger lines and accidentals. Click any notehead to hear it.
  • 🎸 Guitar — Standard or alternate tuning fretboard, 13 frets. Root notes use the primary accent colour. Click any dot to hear the pitch.
  • 🪗 Ukulele — Soprano re-entrant GCEA tuning (or alternate), 12 frets. Same interaction as guitar.

During a quiz, the visualiser shows colour-coded feedback: ■ green for correct, ■ red for wrong. Clicking a wrong note on the visualiser during an active question counts as a miss.

Ear Training Quiz

The quiz plays a random note from your current scale and asks you to identify it by clicking one of the option buttons. After answering, it auto-advances after 1.8 seconds.

  • Play root first — Plays the root note before the mystery note so your ear has a tonal anchor.
  • 10s timer — A countdown ring appears above the prompt. If time runs out, the correct answer is revealed and counts as a timeout. The ring turns red at 3 seconds.
  • Weighted notes — Notes you get wrong appear more frequently in future questions. Correct answers gradually reduce the weighting. This focuses your practice on weak spots automatically.
  • No repeats — The same note is never asked twice in a row (unless the scale only has one unique note).

Score & Reset

The score row shows ✓ correct answers, ✗ wrong answers (including visualiser misclicks), ⏱ timeouts, and an accuracy percentage. It appears once you start answering.

Pressing ✕ Reset shows a summary overlay with encouraging feedback based on your accuracy, then clears everything for a fresh start.

Themes

Five themes are available in the top bar: Dark Gold, Clean & Minimal, Dark Studio, Retro Piano, and Bright & Playful. Each has its own fonts, colours and mood. Your choice is remembered between sessions.

Settings are remembered

All your settings — root note, scale, octave, tuning, visualiser, theme, and toggle states — are saved automatically in your browser and restored the next time you open the app. No account needed.

🎯 Use the Replay button if you missed the note. Use the Staff view while learning to connect sounds with notation.
